Frequently Asked Questions

A sump pump is a device designed to remove water that has accumulated in a water-collecting sump basin, commonly found in the basement of homes. It works by using a float switch to detect rising water levels, which then activates the pump to move water out of the basin through discharge pipes, effectively protecting your basement from flooding.

It is recommended to have your sump pump inspected and tested at least once a year to ensure it is functioning correctly. Regular maintenance helps identify potential issues before they lead to failure, especially before the wet season when the pump will be used more frequently.

Common signs that a sump pump may need repair include unusual noises, constant running, failure to start, visible rust, or the presence of odors. Additionally, if you notice your basement is damp or waterlogged, it may indicate your sump pump is not working efficiently.

While it is possible to install a sump pump yourself if you have plumbing experience, it is often recommended to hire a professional. This ensures that the installation is done correctly, safely, and up to code, reducing the risk of future problems caused by improper installation.

Sump pumps typically last around 7 to 10 years, depending on the model, usage, and maintenance. Regular inspection and maintenance can help extend the lifespan of your sump pump, ensuring it continues to protect your home effectively.
