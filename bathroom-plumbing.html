Frequently Asked Questions

If your bathroom sink is draining slowly, it's often due to a clog caused by hair or soap scum. Start by removing any obvious debris from the drain. You can then use a plunger to try and dislodge the clog. If that doesnâ€™t work, consider using a plumbing snake or a chemical drain cleaner.

A running toilet is often caused by a faulty flapper valve. Inspect the flapper in the tank to see if it is broken or worn out. Replacing it usually solves the problem. Also, check the fill valve and float to ensure they are functioning properly and adjust them if necessary.

Low water pressure in your bathroom sink can be due to clogged aerators, leaks in the water line, or issues with the water supply line. Cleaning the aerator attached to the faucet or checking for visible leaks can help resolve the problem. If the issue persists, consulting a plumber is recommended.

To fix a leaking faucet, first identify the type of faucetâ€”compression, ball, cartridge, or ceramic disk. Shut off the water supply to the faucet and disassemble it to inspect for worn-out washers or seals. Replacing these components often resolves the leak.

Common causes of a clogged toilet include the flushing of non-flushable items, heavy use of toilet paper, and blockages in the trap or drain line. Using a plunger or toilet auger can often clear such clogs, but persistent issues may require professional assistance.
