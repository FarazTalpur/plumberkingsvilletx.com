Frequently Asked Questions

Common causes of kitchen sink clogs include food particles, grease, soap residue, and foreign objects lodged in the drainpipe. Using a strainer and properly disposing of grease and food waste can help prevent clogs.

To fix a leaky kitchen faucet, start by turning off the water supply. Remove the faucet handle and use a wrench to remove the nut. Replace worn washers, O-rings, or cartridges with new ones, and reassemble the faucet.

Regular maintenance includes avoiding pouring grease down the drain, using strainers, cleaning the sink regularly, checking for leaks, and occasionally flushing drains with hot water or a vinegar and baking soda solution.

To unclog a garbage disposal, first ensure that it is turned off. Use a flashlight to check for visible obstructions, remove them using tongs or pliers, and use a hex wrench to manually turn the disposal blades if necessary.

Low water pressure in a kitchen sink can be due to mineral buildup in the aerator, a partially closed valve, or a leak in the plumbing system. Cleaning the aerator and checking valves and pipes can help diagnose the issue.
