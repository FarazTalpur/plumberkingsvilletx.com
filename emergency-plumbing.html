Frequently Asked Questions

First, turn off the main water supply to prevent further flooding. Then, locate the burst pipe and attempt to contain the leak using towels or a bucket. Call a professional emergency plumber immediately for repairs.

For a temporary fix, you can use plumber's tape or a pipe clamp to cover the leak until a plumber arrives. Be sure to shut off the water supply before applying any temporary measures.

Call an emergency plumber when facing issues like burst pipes, overflowing toilets, sewage backups, water heater failures, or any situation causing significant water damage or posing a health hazard.

Regular maintenance is key. Ensure pipes are insulated, avoid pouring grease down drains, check for leaks frequently, and have septic tanks inspected and cleaned as needed.

Keep a plunger, pipe wrench, plumber’s tape, a bucket, and rubber gloves easily accessible. These tools can help manage minor issues until professional help arrives.
