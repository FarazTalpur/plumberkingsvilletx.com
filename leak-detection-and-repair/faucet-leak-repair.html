Frequently Asked Questions

A faucet typically leaks due to worn-out washers, gaskets, or seals. Other common causes include corroded valve seats, loose parts, or high water pressure.

To identify a leaking part, first determine if it's a compression, ball, ceramic-disk, or cartridge faucet. Then, observe where the leak occurs, such as around the handle, spout, or base, which can indicate the faulty component.

You will typically need a wrench set, screwdrivers, pliers, replacement parts (like washers or O-rings), and sometimes a specialized tool like a basin wrench for inaccessible parts.

Yes, many faucet leaks can be fixed by following guides or tutorials, provided they are minor and you have the necessary tools and replacement parts. However, if the problem persists, it's advisable to consult a professional plumber.

Regular maintenance, such as tightening loose parts, replacing worn-out seals, and avoiding excessive force when shutting off the faucet, can prevent leaks. Additionally, installing a water softener can reduce mineral buildup in areas with hard water.
