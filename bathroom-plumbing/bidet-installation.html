Frequently Asked Questions

To install a bidet, you typically need an adjustable wrench, a screwdriver, plumber's tape, and potentially a drill if mounting a standalone bidet.

Many bidet attachments and seats are designed for DIY installation, which most people can handle with basic tools. However, for standalone bidets, you might need a professional for plumbing adjustments.

Bidet attachments and seats are designed to fit most standard two-piece toilets. However, not all models are compatible with one-piece or uniquely shaped toilets.

To prevent leaks, ensure all connections are tight and use plumber's tape on threaded joints. Test the bidet by turning on the water supply slowly and checking for any leaks.

Non-electric bidets do not require an outlet. However, electric bidet seats with heating and additional features usually need a nearby electrical outlet.
