Why Regular Maintenance is Essential

Regular maintenance of your water heater can prevent emergency breakdowns and prolong the unit's lifespan. Here in Kingsville, our experts focus on:

  • Checking Thermostat Settings: Ensuring your water temperature is set correctly to avoid scalding and conserve energy.
  • Inspecting Anode Rods: Replacing worn anode rods to prevent tank corrosion.
  • Flushing the Tank: Removing sediment buildup to improve efficiency and prevent overheating.

Emergency Repair Services

Unexpected issues can occur, and our emergency repair services address:

  • Heating Element Replacement: Fixing faulty heating elements that affect water temperatures.
  • Leak Repairs: Quickly addressing any leaks to avoid water damage.
  • Pressure Relief Valve Checks: Ensuring this safety feature is functioning to prevent overpressure situations.

Tankless Water Heater Maintenance

Frequently Asked Questions

It's recommended to flush your water heater at least once a year to remove sediment buildup and maintain efficiency.

For optimal performance and safety, set your water heater to 120 degrees Fahrenheit. This temperature minimizes the risk of scalding and reduces energy consumption.

Noises from a water heater usually indicate sediment buildup at the bottom of the tank. Regular flushing can help eliminate the sediment and reduce noise.

Inspect the anode rod every couple of years. If it's less than half an inch thick, heavily corroded, or coated with calcium, it's time to replace it to protect the tank from rust.

Signs include inconsistent water temperature, strange noises, leaking around the base, and water that is rusty or has a metallic taste.
