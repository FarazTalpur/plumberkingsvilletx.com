Understanding Backflow Prevention

Backflow occurs when water flows in the reverse direction, potentially allowing contaminants to enter the potable water system. This can pose significant health risks. Backflow prevention devices are installed to keep harmful materials from compromising the water supply.

Why You Need Backflow Testing

  • Regulatory Compliance: Regular backflow testing is often required by state and local regulations to maintain a safe water system.
  • Health Safety: Undetected backflow can introduce bacteria, chemicals, and pollutants into the water, putting your family or customers at risk.
  • Avoid Costly Repairs: Identifying and addressing backflow issues early can prevent costly repairs and water damage in the future.

Frequently Asked Questions

Backflow testing is the process of checking a backflow prevention device to ensure it is functioning properly, preventing contaminated water from flowing back into the clean water supply.

Backflow testing is important because it helps protect the public water supply from contamination by identifying and correcting potential backflow issues.

Backflow testing is typically required annually, but the frequency can vary based on local regulations or the specific requirements of a water supplier.

Backflow testing should be performed by a certified backflow tester who has the necessary training and equipment to conduct the testing and maintenance of backflow prevention devices.

If a backflow test fails, it is necessary to have the device repaired or replaced to ensure the water supply is protected. This corrective action should be completed by a professional to ensure compliance with health and safety regulations.
